# DGCP™ Research Base / Node Concept #0011

## Alaska Node

### Arctic & Cold Region Systems Research Base

**Record ID:** RNC-000011

---

## Concept Statement

> Observe the extremes.  
> Understand adaptation.  
> Respect the land.  
> Build for the future.

DGCP™ Research Base / Node Concept #0011 explores a long-term research base inspired by the cold-region environments of Southcentral Alaska.

The concept investigates how glacial systems, permafrost, cold-climate ecosystems, renewable energy, indigenous knowledge, and environmental resilience may coexist within one integrated research environment.

---

**Reference Location**

Southcentral Alaska, United States

- seasonal daylight variation
- remote wilderness

## Architectural Concept

The architectural language emphasizes resilience in cold environments while maintaining harmony with surrounding landscapes.

Design principles include:

- respect extreme environments
- maximize natural daylight
- highly insulated structures
- elevated foundations
- renewable energy integration
- durable cold-climate materials
- minimal ecological disturbance

The architecture supports long-term scientific observation while remaining adapted to Arctic environmental conditions.
