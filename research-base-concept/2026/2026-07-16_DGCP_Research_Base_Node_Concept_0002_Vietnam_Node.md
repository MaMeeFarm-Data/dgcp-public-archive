# DGCP™ Research Base / Node Concept #0002

## Vietnam Node

### Northern Vietnam Research Base

**Record ID:** RNC-000002

**Concept Role:** Connected Economic & Infrastructure Systems Research Base

**Status:** Concept Only

---

## Concept Statement

> Observe the systems.  
> Understand the connections.  
> Preserve the evidence.

DGCP™ Research Base / Node Concept #0002 explores a long-term research base within the regional context of Northern Vietnam.

The concept is designed around the observation and understanding of connected systems, including infrastructure, logistics, economic activity, industrial development, environmental conditions, resilience, data, evidence, and governance.

Unlike Concept #0001, which explores a tropical integrated systems research environment in Chanthaburi, Thailand, the Vietnam Node develops its own architectural and operational identity in response to the cultural, climatic, and regional characteristics of Northern Vietnam.

The concept combines a calm research environment with access to major economic, industrial, logistics, and infrastructure networks.

---

## Concept Role

The Vietnam Node concept may support observation and research related to:

- connected systems observation;
- infrastructure and logistics;
- economic and industrial systems;
- environmental conditions and resilience;
- data, proof, and governance.

The base is positioned conceptually as a place for long-term observation, documentation, analysis, and understanding of interconnected regional systems.

---

## Site Context

**Reference Zone:** Inland Eastern Corridor, Northern Vietnam

**Example Reference Area:** Hung Yen Province

The concept explores an inland location with potential connections to major regional systems while remaining outside the most intensive urban environment.

The reference context includes:

- connection to Hanoi;
- access toward Hai Phong Port;
- access toward Quang Ninh and Ha Long;
- proximity to highways and transport infrastructure;
- proximity to rail and industrial zones;
- an agricultural and rural setting;
- access to major economic corridors;
- a quieter environment suitable for long-term research.

Conceptual travel relationships shown within the visual study include:

- Hanoi: approximately 45–60 minutes;
- Hai Phong Port: approximately 60–90 minutes;
- Quang Ninh / Ha Long: approximately 90–120 minutes.

These locations and travel times are conceptual reference information only and would require independent site-specific verification.

The concept also prioritizes, where possible:

- safe elevation;
- lower flood-risk conditions;
- long-term accessibility;
- connection without requiring a dense urban location.

---

## Regional Connection

The Vietnam Node is conceptually positioned within a regional network connecting:

- Hanoi;
- Hung Yen;
- Hai Phong;
- Hai Phong Port;
- Quang Ninh;
- Ha Long Bay.

This creates a research context where economic activity, logistics, infrastructure, industrial development, agriculture, environmental systems, and regional connectivity can be observed within a connected geographic area.

The objective is not to place the base directly inside the busiest economic center.

The concept instead explores a position that remains connected to major systems while preserving a quieter environment for research and long-term work.

## Architectural Concept

**Modern Vietnamese • Tropical • Sustainable**

The architectural concept is based on four principles.

### Respect Local Culture

The design draws conceptual inspiration from Vietnamese architecture through:

- sloped roofs;
- deep eaves;
- open spaces;
- natural materials;
- internal courtyards.

The objective is not to reproduce historical architecture directly.

The concept explores how local architectural principles may inform a contemporary research environment.

### Adapt to Climate

The building concept responds to:

- heavy rainfall;
- typhoon conditions;
- humidity;
- heat;
- the need for natural ventilation.

Climate-responsive elements may include:

- deep eaves;
- sloped roofs;
- cross ventilation;
- high ceilings;
- rainwater management;
- elevated floor levels;
- shaded transition spaces.

### Blend with Nature

The concept uses a material and landscape language based on:

- wood;
- brick;
- stone;
- bamboo;
- vegetation;
- courtyards;
- water elements.

### Research in Harmony

The base is intended to provide a calm environment for:

- deep thinking;
- observation;
- documentation;
- analysis;
- long-term research.

Supports heat reduction and interior comfort.

Supports greater water independence.

Provides conceptual resilience against local water conditions.

Creates separation and protection from surrounding roads and external activity.
