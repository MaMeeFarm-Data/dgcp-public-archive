# DGCP™ Research Base / Node Concept #0003

## Queensland Node

### Darling Downs Research Base

**Record ID:** RNC-000003

**Concept Role:** Land • Agricultural • Environmental • Systems Research Base

**Status:** Concept Only

---

## Concept Statement

> Observe land.  
> Understand cycles.  
> Preserve the balance.  
> Build for the future.

DGCP™ Research Base / Node Concept #0003 explores a long-term research base within the conceptual landscape of the Darling Downs region of Queensland, Australia.

The concept is shaped by open land, agricultural systems, environmental conditions, water management, biodiversity, rural infrastructure, regional logistics, and the relationship between human activity and the landscape.

Unlike the previous concepts, the Queensland Node develops around a broader land-based research environment.

The base is not conceived only as a building.

It is conceived as part of a wider working landscape where land, water, food production, biodiversity, livestock, infrastructure, research, and long-term observation interact.

---

The Queensland Node concept may support observation and research related to:

- land and soil systems;

The concept combines a long-term research environment with direct access to land-based systems and regional conditions.

All location, distance, access, elevation, climate, and site information remains conceptual and would require independent verification before real-world use.

## Architectural Concept

The architectural concept is based on five principles.

### Respect the Land

The design explores:

- a low building profile;
- long horizontal lines;
- adaptation to the landscape;
- minimal visual impact.

The architecture is intended to sit within the landscape rather than dominate it.

### Climate Responsive

The concept considers:

- wide verandahs;
- deep eaves;
- cross ventilation;
- shaded living areas;
- strong sunlight;
- seasonal temperature variation.

### Built for Resilience

The concept explores resilience through:

- cyclone-rated structural considerations where applicable;
- bushfire-aware design;
- water systems;
- energy independence;
- operational redundancy.

Actual engineering and hazard requirements would depend on the final site and applicable regulations.

### Local Materials, Modern Expression

The architectural language combines:

- timber;
- stone;
- corrugated metal;
- natural textures;
- contemporary forms.

The objective is a practical and timeless research environment connected to the regional landscape.

### Indoor–Outdoor Living

The design maintains a strong connection between:

- interior spaces;
- verandahs;
- landscape;
- natural light;
- air;
- open views.

## Climate and Land Strategy

The concept explores several climate and land strategies.

### Strong Sun and UV Protection

Building orientation, roof design, verandahs, and shading support protection from intense sunlight.

### Capture Breeze and Maximize Shade

Cross ventilation and shaded outdoor spaces support passive comfort.

### Rainwater Harvesting and Large Storage

The concept considers substantial rainwater capture and storage capacity.

### Drought Resilience and Water Efficiency

Water systems are treated as a central component of the site rather than a secondary utility.

### Bushfire Awareness

The concept includes a conceptual asset protection zone and landscape management considerations.

### Healthy Soil, Cover Crops, and Biodiversity

The wider land strategy explores soil health, vegetation cover, biodiversity, and productive landscapes.

These strategies remain conceptual and would require site-specific environmental, engineering, planning, and regulatory assessment.
