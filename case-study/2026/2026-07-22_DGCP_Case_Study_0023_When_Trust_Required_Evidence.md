# DGCP™ Case Study #0023

## When Trust Required Evidence

### How Verification Changed the Way Humans Build Trust

> *"Trust may begin with a claim. It becomes stronger when supported by verifiable evidence."*

## Purpose

This case study examines how trust develops when claims, promises, or commitments are supported by verifiable evidence. It explores the relationship between evidence, independent verification, confidence, decisions, actions, and updated outcomes. The objective is to observe how modern systems increasingly rely on records, documentation, traceability, and observable evidence as complexity grows.

---

## Core Observation

Trust can begin through reputation, relationships, experience, or expertise. These foundations remain important, but increasingly complex systems often require additional evidence to support decisions and reduce uncertainty.

A claim may be supported by data, documents, records, or other forms of proof. Verification then checks whether that evidence is reliable, consistent, and independently testable. When verification strengthens confidence, people and systems can make decisions and take action with a clearer understanding of uncertainty and risk.

Actions subsequently produce results, feedback, and new evidence. Trust may increase, decrease, or stabilize as this evidence is observed. Trust is therefore not static; it develops continuously as claims and outcomes are compared over time.

---

## Key Points

- Trust may begin before formal evidence is available.
- Claims become more testable when supported by records or proof.
- Independent verification can reduce uncertainty and bias.
- Traceability connects decisions and actions to observable outcomes.
- Weak verification can make trust fragile and systems inefficient.
- New evidence continuously updates the level of trust.

---

## Key Insight

Verification does not replace trust. It helps trust become more resilient by connecting claims with evidence, decisions with actions, and expectations with observable outcomes.
