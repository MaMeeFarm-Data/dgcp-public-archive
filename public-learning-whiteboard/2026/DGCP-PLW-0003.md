## Purpose

This public learning whiteboard explains why recorded data should not be confused with reality itself.

The objective is to help learners understand that data represents only selected observations collected through specific methods, tools, and contexts.

## Reality

Reality is complex, dynamic, and continuously changing.

Many details exist beyond what we can observe or measure.

---

## Observation

Observation captures only a portion of reality through human senses or measuring instruments.

Every observation is selective.

---

## Data

Data is the recorded representation of observations.

It is a simplified snapshot rather than the complete reality.

---

## Analysis

Analysis examines recorded data to identify patterns, relationships, and possible explanations.

The quality of analysis depends on the quality of observations and recorded data.

---

## Understanding

Understanding develops by interpreting analytical results within the appropriate real-world context.

Good understanding recognizes the limitations of available data.

---

## Why Data Is Not Reality

Data is:

- Filtered
- Limited
- Collected with purpose
- Shaped by tools and methods
- Dependent on context

Therefore, data is a representation of reality rather than reality itself.

---

## Examples

Examples where data differs from reality include:

- Temperature measurements
- Sales records
- Survey responses
- GPS location data

Each dataset captures only part of the complete real-world situation.

---

## Key Message

The better our observations and recorded data, the closer we move toward understanding reality.

However, reality is always richer and more complex than any dataset can fully represent.
