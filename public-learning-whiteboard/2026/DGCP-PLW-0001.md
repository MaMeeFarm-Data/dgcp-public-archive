## Purpose

This public learning whiteboard introduces a simple framework showing how observations from the real world become meaningful understanding through structured analysis.

The objective is to help learners distinguish between reality, observation, recorded data, analytical processes, identified patterns, and human understanding.

## Reality

The real world exists independently of our observations.

Many events occur whether or not they are recorded.

---

## Observation

Observation is the process of noticing, measuring, or documenting aspects of reality.

---

## Data

Data is the recorded representation of observations.

Data itself is not reality.

---

## Analysis

Analysis organizes, cleans, explores, and evaluates recorded data to extract useful information.

---

## Pattern

Patterns reveal relationships, trends, similarities, or anomalies discovered through analysis.

---

## Understanding

Understanding emerges when identified patterns are interpreted within the appropriate real-world context.

Understanding supports better decisions and actions.

---

## Key Ingredients

- Statistics
- Computing
- Domain Knowledge

Each component contributes to transforming recorded observations into meaningful understanding.

---

## Core Message

Data is not reality itself.

It is a recorded representation of what we observed.
