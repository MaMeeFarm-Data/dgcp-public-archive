## Purpose

This public learning whiteboard explains the meaning of observation and its role as the bridge between reality and reliable data.

The objective is to help learners understand that observation requires intention, documentation, and evidence rather than assumptions or memory alone.

## Reality

Reality exists independently of our observations.

Events continue whether or not we notice or record them.

---

## Observation

Observation is the intentional process of noticing, measuring, or documenting something from reality.

Observation requires purpose rather than simply seeing.

---

## Why Observe?

Observation reduces uncertainty.

It creates evidence that can later be verified and analyzed.

Reliable understanding begins with reliable observation.

---

## Good Observation

Good observations are:

- Clear
- Repeatable
- Measurable
- Documented

These characteristics improve the quality and usefulness of collected data.

---

## Common Mistakes

Common observation errors include:

- Guessing instead of observing
- Recording opinions as facts
- Relying only on memory
- Failing to document evidence

Good observation depends on recorded evidence rather than assumptions.

---

## Types of Observation

- Visual
- Measurement
- Auditory
- Recorded Documentation

Different forms of observation may be combined to strengthen evidence.

---

## Key Message

Observation is the bridge between reality and data.

Without observation, there is no reliable evidence to analyze.

Good observation today becomes valuable evidence tomorrow.
