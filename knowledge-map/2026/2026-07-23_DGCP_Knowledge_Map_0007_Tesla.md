## Purpose

This educational knowledge map presents a structural overview of Tesla and illustrates how the company integrates electric vehicles, sustainable energy, artificial intelligence, battery technology, software, manufacturing, and global infrastructure into a connected innovation ecosystem.

The objective is to simplify Tesla's overall business architecture into an accessible visual learning resource.

## Core Learning Areas

### Electric Vehicle Platform

Tesla develops a portfolio of electric vehicles including Model S, Model 3, Model X, Model Y, Cybertruck, and future vehicle platforms designed around software-defined engineering.

---

### Sustainable Energy

Tesla expands beyond vehicles through:

- Solar Panels
- Solar Roof
- Powerwall
- Megapack
- Energy Storage Systems

These products support renewable energy generation and energy management.

---

### Artificial Intelligence

Tesla develops AI technologies including:

- Autopilot
- Full Self-Driving (FSD)
- Computer Vision
- Neural Networks
- AI Training Systems

These technologies support driving assistance and intelligent vehicle capabilities.

---

### Manufacturing & Battery Technology

Tesla emphasizes:

- Gigafactories
- Vertical Integration
- Battery Innovation
- Supply Chain Optimization
- Cost Efficiency

Its manufacturing strategy combines hardware, software, and production engineering.

---

### Software & Connectivity

Tesla vehicles receive continuous improvements through:

- Over-the-Air Software Updates
- Tesla Mobile App
- Real-time Connectivity
- Cloud Infrastructure
- Fleet Learning

Software remains a central component of the overall product ecosystem.

---

### Customer Value

Tesla focuses on providing:

- Electric mobility
- Sustainable energy solutions
- Intelligent vehicle technology
- Fast charging infrastructure
- Continuous software improvement
- Integrated ecosystem experiences

---

## Educational Perspective

This knowledge map demonstrates how Tesla combines electric vehicles, renewable energy, artificial intelligence, software engineering, manufacturing, and infrastructure into a connected technology ecosystem.

It emphasizes structural relationships between energy, transportation, software, manufacturing, and sustainability rather than commercial recommendations.
