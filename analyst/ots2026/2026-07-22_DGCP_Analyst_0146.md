### System Context

Attribution remains one of the most important yet least visible capabilities supporting modern civilization.

Infrastructure depends on attribution.

Organizations depend on attribution.

Institutions depend on attribution.

Civilization depends on systems capable of preserving the origin of knowledge, information, assets, decisions, and operational activities.

Many observers focus on authorship or ownership.

The discussion frequently centers on giving credit.

The structural capability allowing systems to permanently preserve provenance and responsibility across interconnected operations receives less attention.

While reviewing developments across infrastructure, governance, healthcare, finance, logistics, manufacturing, scientific research, and digital systems, I found myself paying less attention to names attached to work and more attention to the attribution allowing systems to preserve origin through trustworthy evidence.

The names remained visible.

The preserved origin became visible.

The observation was not about recognition.

The observation was about preserving origin.
