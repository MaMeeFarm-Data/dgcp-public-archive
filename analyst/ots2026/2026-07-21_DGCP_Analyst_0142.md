### System Context

Auditability remains one of the most important yet least visible capabilities supporting modern civilization.

Infrastructure depends on auditability.

Organizations depend on auditability.

Institutions depend on auditability.

Civilization depends on systems capable of independently verifying operational integrity through trustworthy evidence.

Many observers focus on audits as periodic inspections.

The discussion frequently centers on compliance activities.

The structural capability allowing systems to be continuously examined, verified, and trusted through observable evidence receives less attention.

While reviewing developments across infrastructure, governance, healthcare, finance, logistics, manufacturing, scientific research, and digital systems, I found myself paying less attention to audit procedures and more attention to the auditability allowing complex systems to demonstrate integrity without relying solely on human assertion.

The audit process remained visible.

The verifiable integrity became visible.

The observation was not about inspections.

The observation was about trustworthy verification.
