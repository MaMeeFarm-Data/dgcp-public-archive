### System Context

Correction remains one of the most important yet least visible mechanisms supporting modern civilization.

Infrastructure requires correction.

Organizations require correction.

Institutions require correction.

Civilization depends on corrective systems.

Many observers focus on mistakes, errors, or failures.

The discussion frequently centers on what went wrong.

The systems restoring stability through continuous correction receive less attention.

While reviewing developments across infrastructure, governance, manufacturing, healthcare, finance, logistics, scientific research, and digital systems, I found myself paying less attention to individual errors and more attention to the corrective mechanisms allowing systems to maintain reliable operation over time.

The deviation remained visible.

The stability infrastructure became visible.

The observation was not about mistakes alone.

The observation was about maintaining stability.
