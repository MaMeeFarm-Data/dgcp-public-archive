### System Context

Feedback remains one of the most important yet least visible mechanisms supporting modern civilization.

Infrastructure depends on feedback.

Organizations depend on feedback.

Institutions depend on feedback.

Civilization depends on continuous feedback systems.

Many observers focus on responses after an event has occurred.

The discussion frequently centers on individual reactions or outcomes.

The systems enabling continuous adjustment receive less attention.

While reviewing developments across infrastructure, governance, healthcare, finance, manufacturing, logistics, scientific research, and digital systems, I found myself paying less attention to isolated responses and more attention to the feedback mechanisms allowing systems to observe, adjust, and continue operating over time.

The response remained visible.

The self-regulating infrastructure became visible.

The observation was not about reaction alone.

The observation was about continuous adjustment.
