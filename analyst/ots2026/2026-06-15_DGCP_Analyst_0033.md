### System Context

Shipping remains one of the most important foundations of the global economy.

Raw materials move through shipping.

Energy resources move through shipping.

Industrial products move through shipping.

Agricultural products move through shipping.

International trade depends heavily on shipping.

Many observers focus on vessels, freight rates, shipping companies, or maritime markets.

The discussion frequently centers on transportation activity itself.

The systems enabled by maritime transportation receive less attention.

While reviewing developments across global trade, manufacturing, energy systems, logistics networks, and economic development, I found myself paying less attention to ships and more attention to the infrastructure operating through them.

The ship remained visible.

The trade infrastructure became visible.

The observation was not about transportation alone.

The observation was about connectivity.
