### System Context

Systems remain among the most important yet least visible foundations supporting modern civilization.

Organizations operate as systems.

Infrastructure operates as systems.

Governance operates as systems.

Civilization operates through interconnected systems.

Many observers focus on individual organizations, technologies, or institutions.

The discussion frequently centers on separate components.

The relationships connecting those components receive less attention.

While reviewing developments across governments, industries, infrastructure, science, finance, and digital networks, I found myself paying less attention to individual structures and more attention to the systems connecting them.

The component remained visible.

The interconnected infrastructure became visible.

The observation was not about individual parts.

The observation was about relationships.
