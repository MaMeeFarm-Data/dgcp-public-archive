### System Context

Agriculture remains one of the oldest and most essential systems supporting human civilization.

Food originates from agriculture.

Livestock systems depend on agriculture.

Food industries depend on agriculture.

Communities depend on food systems.

Civilizations depend on reliable food availability.

Many observers focus on farms, crops, harvests, commodity prices, or agricultural markets.

The discussion frequently centers on production itself.

The systems supported by agricultural production receive less attention.

While reviewing developments across food systems, population growth, trade networks, resource management, and economic development, I found myself paying less attention to farming activities and more attention to the infrastructure operating through them.

The farm remained visible.

The civilization infrastructure became visible.

The observation was not about farming alone.

The observation was about continuity.
