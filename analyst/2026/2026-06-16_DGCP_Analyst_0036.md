### System Context

Fertilizer remains one of the most important but least visible inputs supporting modern food systems.

Crop production depends on nutrients.

Agricultural productivity depends on nutrients.

Food systems depend on agricultural productivity.

Modern populations depend on food systems.

Many observers focus on farms, harvests, food prices, or agricultural markets.

The discussion frequently centers on food production itself.

The systems enabling food production receive less attention.

While reviewing developments across agriculture, food security, resource systems, and global trade, I found myself paying less attention to crops and more attention to the infrastructure supporting their growth.

The fertilizer remained visible.

The productivity infrastructure became visible.

The observation was not about agriculture alone.

The observation was about dependency.

Agricultural policy influences nutrient access.

No single institution controls every contributing factor.

Multiple systems interact continuously.
