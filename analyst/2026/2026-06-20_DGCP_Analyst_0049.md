### System Context

Payment systems remain among the most important yet least visible systems supporting modern civilization.

People make payments.

Businesses receive payments.

Governments process payments.

Markets depend on payments.

Commerce depends on payments.

Many observers focus on transactions, banking applications, payment cards, digital wallets, or financial technology.

The discussion frequently centers on individual transactions.

The systems enabling exchange receive less attention.

While reviewing developments across commerce, banking, digital economies, international trade, and financial infrastructure, I found myself paying less attention to transactions and more attention to the infrastructure operating through them.

The payment remained visible.

The exchange infrastructure became visible.

The observation was not about transactions alone.

The observation was about continuity.
