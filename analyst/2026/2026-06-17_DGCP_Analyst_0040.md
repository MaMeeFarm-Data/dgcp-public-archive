### System Context

Cold chains remain one of the least visible yet most important systems supporting modern civilization.

Food preservation depends on cold chains.

Medical logistics depend on cold chains.

Vaccine distribution depends on cold chains.

Temperature-sensitive products depend on cold chains.

Many observers focus on food products, refrigerated vehicles, storage facilities, or retail distribution.

The discussion frequently centers on products themselves.

The systems preserving those products receive less attention.

While reviewing developments across food systems, healthcare logistics, distribution networks, and global trade, I found myself paying less attention to products and more attention to the infrastructure preserving their usability.

The refrigeration remained visible.

The preservation infrastructure became visible.

The observation was not about cooling alone.

The observation was about continuity.
