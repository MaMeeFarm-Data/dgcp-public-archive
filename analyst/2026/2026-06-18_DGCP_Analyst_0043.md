### System Context

Housing remains one of the most fundamental requirements supporting modern civilization.

People require housing.

Families require housing.

Workers require housing.

Communities require housing.

Urban systems require housing.

Many observers focus on property markets, construction activity, housing prices, or real-estate development.

The discussion frequently centers on buildings.

The systems supported by housing receive less attention.

While reviewing developments across labor markets, urban growth, demographic trends, infrastructure systems, and economic development, I found myself paying less attention to buildings and more attention to the infrastructure operating through them.

The house remained visible.

The population infrastructure became visible.

The observation was not about property alone.

The observation was about continuity.
