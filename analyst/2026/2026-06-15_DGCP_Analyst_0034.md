### System Context

Containers remain among the most influential but least discussed innovations within modern trade systems.

Goods move in containers.

Ships carry containers.

Trains carry containers.

Trucks carry containers.

Ports process containers.

Many observers focus on products, shipping activity, trade volumes, or transportation services.

The discussion frequently centers on cargo movement itself.

The systems enabling coordinated movement receive less attention.

While reviewing developments across global logistics, shipping, manufacturing, and supply-chain operations, I found myself paying less attention to goods and more attention to the infrastructure enabling those goods to move efficiently.

The container remained visible.

The standardization infrastructure became visible.

The observation was not about cargo alone.

The observation was about coordination.
