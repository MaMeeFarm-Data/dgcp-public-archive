### System Context

Cities remain among the most important organizational structures supporting modern civilization.

People live in cities.

Businesses operate in cities.

Institutions operate in cities.

Services operate in cities.

Economic activity concentrates in cities.

Many observers focus on buildings, transportation, economic growth, population density, or urban lifestyles.

The discussion frequently centers on cities as locations.

The systems operating through cities receive less attention.

While reviewing developments across infrastructure, housing, transportation, labor markets, public services, and economic development, I found myself paying less attention to cities as places and more attention to the infrastructure operating through them.

The city remained visible.

The civilization infrastructure became visible.

The observation was not about geography alone.

The observation was about coordination.
