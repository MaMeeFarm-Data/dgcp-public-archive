### System Context

Redundancy remains one of the most important yet least visible foundations supporting modern civilization.

Power systems use redundancy.

Communication systems use redundancy.

Transportation systems use redundancy.

Financial systems use redundancy.

Infrastructure systems use redundancy.

Many observers focus on efficiency, optimization, cost reduction, or operational performance.

The discussion frequently centers on eliminating duplication.

The systems protected by duplication receive less attention.

While reviewing developments across infrastructure networks, energy systems, telecommunications, logistics systems, and digital platforms, I found myself paying less attention to efficiency and more attention to the infrastructure supporting continuity when primary systems fail.

The duplication remained visible.

The continuity infrastructure became visible.

The observation was not about excess capacity alone.

The observation was about survivability.
