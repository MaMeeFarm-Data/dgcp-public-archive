### System Context

Healthcare remains one of the most important systems supporting modern civilization.

People require healthcare.

Families require healthcare.

Workers require healthcare.

Communities require healthcare.

Population systems require healthcare continuity.

Many observers focus on hospitals, doctors, treatments, medical costs, or healthcare services.

The discussion frequently centers on medical activity itself.

The systems supported by healthcare receive less attention.

While reviewing developments across population systems, labor markets, economic activity, public services, and social structures, I found myself paying less attention to healthcare facilities and more attention to the infrastructure operating through them.

The hospital remained visible.

The human continuity infrastructure became visible.

The observation was not about treatment alone.

The observation was about continuity.
