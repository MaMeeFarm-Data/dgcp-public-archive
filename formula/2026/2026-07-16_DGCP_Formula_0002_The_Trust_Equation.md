# DGCP™ Formula #0002

## The Trust Equation

![DGCP Formula 0002 — The Trust Equation](https://ivory-advanced-sparrow-679.mypinata.cloud/ipfs/bafybeif4axiu4zwyfqzq2oulw2g63zl6camiw5ar2m4rxtwtusqpktcemq)

---

## Core Formula

Verified Proof

−

Unresolved Error

↓

Integrated over Time

↓

Accumulated Trust

---

## Formula Expression

\[
T(t) = \int_{0}^{t} \left[P(\tau) - E_r(\tau)\right] d\tau
\]

Where:

- \(T(t)\) = Accumulated Trust at time \(t\)
- \(P(\tau)\) = Verified Proof observed over time
- \(E_r(\tau)\) = Unresolved Error observed over time
- \(t\) = Time
- \(\tau\) = Time variable used within the integration

This is a conceptual formula representing trust as the accumulated result of verified proof minus unresolved error over time.

---

## 1. Trust

Trust represents accumulated credibility.

Within this conceptual model, trust is not created by a single statement, promise, or event.

It develops through repeated evidence, consistent records, and the responsible handling of errors.

\[
Trust = Accumulated\ Credibility
\]

---

## 2. Verified Proof

Verified proof includes observable, logged, traceable, and reviewable evidence.

Within the conceptual formula:

\[
P \uparrow \Rightarrow T \uparrow
\]

More verified proof may strengthen trust when the supporting records remain consistent and reviewable.

The value of proof depends on its quality, context, provenance, and verification.

---

## 3. Unresolved Error

Unresolved error includes unsupported information, uncorrected records, unexplained inconsistencies, and identified problems that remain unaddressed.

Within the conceptual formula:

\[
E_r \uparrow \Rightarrow T \downarrow
\]

An error does not automatically destroy trust.

However, repeated or unresolved errors may weaken accumulated credibility.

Transparent correction may reduce the long-term effect of an identified error.

---

## 4. Time

Time allows proof, errors, corrections, and consistency to accumulate.

Trust may develop slowly because credibility requires repeated observation.

Trust may decline quickly when significant errors remain unresolved.

\[
T(t) = \int_{0}^{t} Net\ Credibility\ d\tau
\]

Time does not create trust by itself.

It provides the period across which behavior and evidence can be observed.

---

## How It Works

More verified proof may increase trust.

\[
P \uparrow \Rightarrow T \uparrow
\]

More unresolved error may decrease trust.

\[
E_r \uparrow \Rightarrow T \downarrow
\]

Consistent proof can accumulate gradually.

\[
\int_{0}^{t} P(\tau)d\tau \uparrow
\]

Unresolved errors can reduce accumulated credibility.

\[
\int_{0}^{t} E_r(\tau)d\tau \uparrow
\]

The resulting trust level depends on the balance between verified proof and unresolved error across time.

---

## Trust over Time

When verified proof continues to accumulate and unresolved errors remain limited:

\[
P(\tau) > E_r(\tau)
\]

Trust may increase over time.

When unresolved errors repeatedly exceed verified proof:

\[
E_r(\tau) > P(\tau)
\]

Trust may decline.

The structural relationship can be summarized as:

\[
Continuous\ Proof \rightarrow Accumulated\ Trust
\]

\[
Unresolved\ Error \rightarrow Reduced\ Trust
\]

---

## Observation

Trust cannot be reliably created through claims alone.

Trust cannot be separated from history.

In a well-maintained, append-only record system, changes and corrections should remain traceable.

Time preserves opportunities to compare claims with evidence.

Proof remains useful when its provenance and context remain reviewable.

---

## Hypothesis

Trust is accumulated.

It cannot simply be downloaded.

It must be integrated over time.

\[
Trust \neq Instant\ Claim
\]

\[
Trust = Evidence + Consistency + Time
\]

This is a conceptual relationship rather than a validated mathematical identity.

---

## Error and Correction

An identified error should not automatically be hidden or removed from history.

A transparent correction creates a traceable relationship between the original record and the corrected information.

Within the conceptual model:

\[
Resolved\ Error < Unresolved\ Error
\]

Correction does not erase the past.

It demonstrates how the system responds when a problem is identified.

---

## Key Takeaway

Trust is not built by promises alone.

Trust is built through continuous proof.

Verified evidence strengthens credibility.

Unresolved error weakens credibility.

Time reveals how consistently both are handled.

---

## A Little Humor

**Promise:** "Trust me immediately."

**Proof:** "I brought the records."

**Error:** "Are you going to correct me?"

**Time:** "I will remember what everyone did." 

---

## Learning Point

Trust is earned through observable conduct.

Proof provides support.

Correction demonstrates accountability.

Time allows consistency to become visible.

A trustworthy system does not need to claim perfection.

It needs to preserve evidence and respond transparently when errors occur.

---

## DGCP™ Observation

Continuous proof strengthens the ability to evaluate credibility over time.

Trust becomes structurally reviewable when evidence, errors, corrections, and timestamps remain connected.

The objective is not to claim that a system never makes mistakes.

The objective is to ensure that its history remains traceable when mistakes occur.
