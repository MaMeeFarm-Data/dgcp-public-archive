## What Is Observation?

## Core Formula

Reality

↓

Purposeful Attention

↓

Observation

↓

Documentation

↓

Evidence

↓

Data

↓

Understanding

---

## Formula Expression

\[
Observation = Purposeful\ Attention\ to\ Reality
\]

A more complete observation model can be expressed as:

\[
O = P + A + M + D
\]

Where:

- \(O\) = Observation
- \(P\) = Purpose
- \(A\) = Attention
- \(M\) = Measurement
- \(D\) = Documentation

The resulting structural relationship can be summarized as:

\[
Better\ Observation \rightarrow Better\ Data \rightarrow Better\ Understanding
\]

---

## 1. Reality

Reality exists independently of whether it is observed.

Events continue whether people notice them or not.

Observation does not create reality.

It helps us understand it.

---

## 2. Observation

Observation is the intentional process of directing attention toward reality.

Seeing alone is not always sufficient.

Useful observation requires purpose, attention, measurement, and documentation.

---

## 3. Why Observe?

Observation reduces uncertainty.

It creates evidence that can later be reviewed, compared, and verified.

Without observation, reliable analysis becomes difficult.

---

## 4. Good Observation

A useful observation should be:

- Purposeful
- Clear
- Measurable
- Repeatable
- Documented

These qualities improve reliability and future usability.

---

## 5. Common Mistakes

Guessing is not observation.

Opinion is not evidence.

Memory is not documented observation.

\[
Guessing \neq Observation
\]

\[
Opinion \neq Evidence
\]

\[
Memory \neq Recorded\ Observation
\]

---

## Key Idea

Observation connects reality to data.

\[
Reality \xrightarrow{Observation} Data
\]

Without observation, there is little reliable information available for analysis.

---

## Types of Observation

### Visual Observation

What we see.

### Measurement

What we measure using instruments, sensors, scales, or defined methods.

### Auditory Observation

What we hear.

### Recorded Observation

What we preserve in a form that can later be reviewed and verified.

## Learning Point

Good observation today may become valuable evidence tomorrow.

The quality of future data depends on the clarity, consistency, and traceability of today's observations.

Better observation supports better data.

Better data supports better understanding.

---

## DGCP™ Observation

Observation becomes structurally useful when it is recorded with sufficient context to be reviewed, verified, and understood.

Purpose determines what receives attention.

Measurement determines how reality is described.

Documentation preserves observation beyond human memory.
